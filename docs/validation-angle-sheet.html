Comeback — Knee Angle Validation Sheet (task 1.6)

Open in a browser and print at 100% scale (no "fit to page"). Landscape. This is your free goniometer.

What this is: a printed protractor that measures the interior knee angle — the angle between your thigh and your shin.

  1. Cut out the protractor (or tape the whole sheet to a wall / a piece of card).
  2. Put the centre dot on the side of your knee joint.
  3. Line the 0–180 baseline up along your thigh.
  4. Read where your shin crosses the scale. That's the interior angle.
  5. Bend to a few set angles (try 180°, 135°, 90°, 45°) and at each one, write down BOTH numbers: the protractor reading and the phone's reading.

Important — two different numbers:

This protractor reads the interior angle (straight leg = 180°).
The app reports flexion (straight leg = 0°).
flexion = 180 − interior angle.
So protractor 135° ↔ app should read ~45°; protractor 90° ↔ app ~90°.

Also test position sensitivity: hold one real angle steady and read the phone at a few different heights / distances / small tilts. Note how much the number drifts — that's what we standardise against.
